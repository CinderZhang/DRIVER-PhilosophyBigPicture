## Preamble: Why This Research Matters

We stand at an inflection point in human history. For the first time, machines can perform cognitive tasks that previously defined professional expertise. The $15 billion equity research industry, the career trajectories of financial professionals, and the information architecture of capital markets all depend on assumptions about human comparative advantage in financial analysis.

When those assumptions no longer hold, we face first-order questions:
- How do humans develop expertise when AI can perform expert-level tasks?
- What capabilities remain distinctly valuable?
- How should we train the next generation of professionals?

**This research program addresses these questions with empirical evidence, not speculation.**

DRIVER is not merely a teaching methodology—it is a designed intervention that generates unique data on human adaptation to AI augmentation. This master plan outlines the architecture for transforming DRIVER from pedagogical practice to rigorous academic research with publication potential in top finance, management, and education journals.

## Part I: The Intellectual Foundation

### 1.1 The Central Research Question

> **"How do humans develop expertise when AI systems can perform expert-level cognitive tasks?"**

This question sits at the intersection of:
- **Finance**: How does AI change information production and financial labor markets?
- **Management**: How do organizations develop human capital in AI-augmented environments?
- **Cognitive Science**: How do humans learn when cognitive support is always available?
- **Policy**: How should we prepare the workforce for an AI-transformed economy?

### 1.2 The Intellectual Pivot

| FROM | TO |
|------|-----|
| Teaching methodology | Empirical research program |
| Training framework | Field experiment on human capital development |
| Certification system | Evidence base for financial labor market evolution |
| Pedagogical philosophy | Testable hypotheses about AI-human collaboration |
| "DRIVER helps students learn" | "Structured AI collaboration builds capabilities rather than creating dependency" |

### 1.3 The Research Gap

**What exists:**
- AI productivity studies (Brynjolfsson et al., 2024): Measure speed/output, not cognitive development
- Financial education research (Kaiser & Menkhoff, 2020): Modest effects, no AI integration
- Deskilling literature: Documents problems, offers no solutions
- Professional training research: No AI integration, limited labor market tracking

**What's missing:**
- Evidence on whether AI-integrated training builds or erodes cognitive capabilities
- Labor market outcomes linked to AI collaboration training
- Longitudinal tracking of mental model evolution
- Validated measures of AI partnership effectiveness

**DRIVER fills this gap with:**
- 280+ participants across multiple courses
- Longitudinal data (Week 1, 7, 14, +6 months)
- Multi-method measurement (survey, behavioral, performance, qualitative)
- Both cognitive AND career outcomes
- Unique intervention design enabling causal inference

### 1.4 The Core Hypotheses

**Main Effects:**
- **H1**: Structured AI collaboration (DRIVER) builds analytical capabilities rather than creating dependency
- **H2**: DRIVER students achieve better career outcomes than traditionally-trained students
- **H3**: Mental models evolve from "AI as oracle" to "AI as partner" under DRIVER

**Mechanisms:**
- **H4**: Prompt engineering skill development mediates capability building
- **H5**: Metacognitive awareness mediates the DRIVER effect on independent performance
- **H6**: Critical evaluation skills protect against cognitive dependency

**Moderators:**
- **H7**: Deep learning approach amplifies DRIVER effectiveness
- **H8**: Technology acceptance moderates intervention engagement
- **H9**: Prior experience shows inverted-U relationship with outcomes

## Part VIII: Quality Assurance

### 8.1 Threats to Validity

**Internal Validity:**
- Selection bias → Address with PSM + DiD
- Attrition → Track and model
- Instructor effects → Control or randomize
- Hawthorne effects → Include behavioral measures

**External Validity:**
- Single institution → Future multi-site replication
- Finance-specific → Test generalization to other domains
- Student population → Complement with professional samples

**Construct Validity:**
- Custom scales → Pilot test, validate psychometrically
- Multi-method → Triangulate across data sources
- Social desirability → Include behavioral measures

### 8.2 Robustness Checks

- Sensitivity analysis for unobserved confounders
- Alternative matching specifications
- Placebo tests on pre-treatment outcomes
- Subgroup analyses for heterogeneous effects
- Alternative outcome measures

### 8.3 Ethical Considerations

- Informed consent at each timepoint
- Right to withdraw without penalty
- Control group receives AI guidance (ethical obligation)
- Data de-identification
- Transparent reporting of all analyses
